PART 1 — USER MANUAL
Chapter 1

Getting Started 🚀

1.1 The Login Screen

When you open cyrabelldental_v2.html, the first thing you see is the login screen. There are two ways in:

  • Admin Mode — full access (patients, payments, settings).
  • Kiosk Mode — a locked-down screen for patients to check themselves in.
The login screen — type credentials (1,2), then choose Admin or Kiosk.
  1. Type your username in the first box.
  2. Type your password in the second box.
  3. Click Admin for full access, or Kiosk for the patient check-in screen.
💡 Tip Bookmark the app file in your browser so reception can open it with one click each morning.
⚠️ Warning Kiosk Mode hides admin features on purpose. Don't leave a tablet on Admin Mode where patients can reach it.

1.2 The Dashboard & Sidebar

After logging in as Admin, you land on the Dashboard. The dark sidebar on the left is your home base — it never moves and gets you anywhere in two clicks.

Dashboard: the dark sidebar (1) and at-a-glance stat cards (2).

1.3 Understanding the Sidebar Icons

  • 📊 Dashboard — today's numbers at a glance.
  • 👥 Patients — your full patient directory.
  • 📅 Appointments — the booking calendar/list.
  • 💳 Payments — money in and receipts.
  • 🦷 Services — your treatment price list.
  • 🔄 Sync — backup & Google Sheets.
  • ⚙️ Settings — clinic info & dentists.
🔔 Remember The highlighted (teal) sidebar item is the screen you're currently on. Always glance there if you feel lost.
Chapter 2

Managing Patients 👥

2.1 Viewing the Patient List

Click 👥 Patients in the sidebar. You get a searchable table of everyone in the clinic.

Patient list: add new (1) and search (2). Click a row to open a profile.

2.2 Adding a New Patient

  1. Click the yellow + New Patient button (top right).
  2. Fill in Full Name, Phone, Birthdate, Address, and Gender.
  3. Add optional Medical History / Allergies notes.
  4. Click Save. The patient appears instantly in the list.
The New Patient form — fill the fields and hit Save.
💡 Tip Phone number doubles as the contact for SMS reminders later (Chapter 5). Enter it carefully!

2.3 Searching for a Patient

Type any part of a name or phone number into the search box at the top of the Patients screen. The list filters as you type — no Enter key needed.

2.4 The Patient Profile (Tabs)

Click any patient row to open their profile. Across the top are six tabs.

Patient profile with tabs: Info • Dental Chart • Appointments • Payments • Files • History.
  • Info — personal & medical details.
  • Dental Chart — clickable tooth map for treatments.
  • Appointments — this patient's past & future bookings.
  • Payments — their billing & receipts.
  • Files — uploaded X-rays, scans, documents.
  • History — a running activity log.
🔔 Remember The ⚠️ Allergies field shows in red on the Info tab. Always check it before any procedure.
Chapter 3

Appointments 📅

3.1 Viewing Today's Appointments

Click 📅 Appointments. By default you see today, sorted by time, each with a colored status badge.

Today's appointments with color-coded status badges (1).

3.2 Adding a New Appointment

  1. Click + New Appt.
  2. Search and select the patient (or add a new one).
  3. Pick the date, a time slot, the service, and the dentist.
  4. Click Save. It starts as Pending.

3.3 Changing Appointment Status

Open an appointment and use the status dropdown to move it through its life cycle:

Pending → Confirmed → Completed.
⚠️ Warning Only mark an appointment Completed after the patient has actually been seen — completed appointments feed your revenue stats.

3.4 Kiosk Check-In Mode

Log in via Kiosk to turn a tablet into a self-service check-in station. Patients tap their name to announce they've arrived.

Kiosk mode: patients tap their own name to check in.
🌟 Quick Step Kiosk: open app → click Kiosk → hand the tablet to patients. To exit, refresh and log in as Admin.
Chapter 4

Payments 💳

4.1 Viewing Payment Records

Click 💳 Payments for a ledger of all money received, with running totals at the top.

Payments ledger with received vs. pending totals (1).

4.2 Recording a Payment

  1. Click + Record Pay.
  2. Select the patient and the service.
  3. Enter the amount and method (Cash, GCash, Card).
  4. Click Save Payment.

4.3 Paying a Pending Appointment (the Pay Button)

New! When an appointment has an unpaid balance, a yellow Pay button appears right on the appointment. Click it to jump straight to a pre-filled payment form — no re-typing the patient or service.

The new Pay button on a pending appointment.
💡 Tip The Pay button is the fastest way to take money — use it instead of +Record Pay whenever the patient came from an appointment.

4.4 Printing a Receipt

  1. Open any payment record.
  2. Click 🖨️ Print Receipt.
  3. Your browser's print dialog opens with a clean, clinic-branded receipt.
🔔 Remember Set your printer to "Save as PDF" to email receipts to patients instead of printing paper.
Chapter 5

Notifications & SMS 🔔

5.1 Sending Appointment Confirmations

From any appointment, click 📲 Send Confirmation. The app composes a ready-to-send message with the patient's name, date, time, and clinic address.

Auto-composed confirmation message.

5.2 Sending Reminders

The day before, use ⏰ Send Reminder on each appointment, or send them in a batch from the appointment list.

🌟 Quick Step Open appointment → Send Reminder → review text → Send. Done in 5 seconds.
⚠️ Warning SMS only works if the patient's phone number is filled in correctly (Chapter 2). Double-check the digits.
PART 2 — ADMIN MANUAL
Chapter 6

Service Catalog 🦷

6.1 Viewing the Service List

Click 🦷 Services to see every treatment you offer, its category, and its price.

Service catalog: ✏️ edit (1) and 🗑️ delete (2) on each row.

6.2 Adding, Editing & Deleting a Service

  1. Add: click + Add Service, enter name, category & price, then Save.
  2. Edit: click the ✏️ pencil on a row, change the values, Save.
  3. Delete: click the 🗑️ trash icon and confirm.
⚠️ Warning Deleting a service does not erase past payments that used it, but it will no longer appear when booking new appointments.

6.3 Service Categories

Group services into categories (Preventive, Surgery, Restorative, Orthodontics…) so they're easy to find when booking. Pick the category from the dropdown in the Add/Edit form.

Chapter 7

Sync & Backup 🔄

7.1 Setting Up Google Sheets Sync

Open 🔄 Sync. Paste your Google Apps Script URL into the sync field and click Save. This connects the app to a Google Sheet that stores a cloud copy of your data.

Sync screen: Apps Script URL, Push (1), Pull (2), Excel backup & Gemini key.

7.2 Pushing Data to Sheets

Click ⬆ Push to Sheets to upload everything from the app to your Google Sheet. Do this at the end of each day.

7.3 Pulling Data from Sheets

Click ⬇ Pull from Sheets to download the latest cloud copy into the app — handy when starting work on a different device.

⚠️ Warning Pulling overwrites local data with the cloud version. Push first if you have unsaved changes on this device.

7.4 Excel Backup Export / Import

  1. Export: click 📤 Export .xlsx to download a spreadsheet backup.
  2. Import: click 📥 Import .xlsx and choose a previously exported file to restore.
🔔 Remember Keep a weekly Excel backup on a USB drive as a safety net, even if you use Google Sheets sync.

7.5 Setting Up the Gemini OCR Key

Paste your Gemini API key into the OCR field on the Sync screen and Save. This unlocks card scanning in Chapter 8.

💡 Tip Get a free key at Google AI Studio. The key only needs to be entered once per device.
Chapter 8

OCR — Scanning Record Cards 📷

8.1 What OCR Does

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) reads a photo of an old paper patient record card and automatically types the details into a new patient form — saving you from manual data entry.

8.2 How to Scan a Record Card

  1. From the Patients screen, click 📷 Scan Card.
  2. Take a photo or upload an image of the record card.
  3. Wait a few seconds while Gemini reads it.
  4. The extracted fields appear for your review.
OCR turns a photo of a paper card into a filled-in patient form.

8.3 Reviewing Extracted Data

Always read the extracted fields before saving — OCR is smart but not perfect with messy handwriting. Fix anything that looks wrong, then click Save Patient.

⚠️ Warning Double-check the phone number and birthdate — a wrong digit means missed reminders or incorrect records.
Chapter 9

Public Booking Page 🌐

9.1 Sharing the Booking Link

Your clinic has a public page where patients book themselves. Share the link on Facebook, your website, or via QR code at the front desk.

9.2 How Patients Book (3-Step Form)

The public 3-step booking form: details → service → date/time.

9.3 Reviewing New Bookings

Patient bookings arrive as Pending appointments in your Appointments screen, flagged as web bookings. Review, confirm, and send a confirmation SMS (Chapter 5).

🌟 Quick Step Each morning, open Appointments → filter Pending → confirm any new web bookings.
Chapter 10

System Settings ⚙️

10.1 Clinic Info Configuration

Open ⚙️ Settings to set your clinic name, address, phone, and logo. These appear on receipts and SMS messages.

Settings: clinic info, dentist list (Add, 1) and time slots.

10.2 Adding Dentists

  1. Type the dentist's name in the "add dentist" box.
  2. Click Add — they now appear in appointment booking dropdowns.
  3. Click the ✕ next to a name to remove a dentist.

10.3 Appointment Time Slots

Toggle which times are bookable. Teal slots are open; grey slots are closed. This controls what patients see on the public booking page (Chapter 9).

  1. Click a time slot to turn it on (teal) or off (grey).
  2. Click 💾 Save to apply.
🔔 Remember Always click Save after changing settings — nothing is stored until you do.
